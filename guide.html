Modules and Resources

Inside a course, you can navigate to the “Modules” section to find all materials uploaded by teachers (photos, documents, videos, etc.). Pressing a module displays its associated resources.

Tasks and Exams

You can access the “Tasks” and “Exams” sections to view available activities for submission or completion, as well as those already completed or expired.

Course Members

To see who is enrolled in a course, go to the “Members” section. You can view the list of teachers, assistants, and students. Clicking on a member shows their profile and basic info.

Course Forum

Navigate to “More” > “Forum” to see or post questions. You must fill in a title, description, and select tags. You can also attach files.

After posting, you can edit or delete your entries. Questions can be sorted and rated by others. You can reply using the “Write Answer” button.

My Grades

To view your grades from tasks and exams, go to “My Grades” under the “More” menu.

Creating a Module

Go to the “Modules” section and press the “+” button to create a new module. Fill in the module information and save.

Creating a Resource

Inside a module, press the “+” button to create a new resource and complete the resource info.

Creating a Task

Inside the “Tasks” section, press the “+” button. You can create multiple-choice, written or file-attachment questions. Each question can include an image and be removed if needed.

Press “Create Task” when done. This same process applies to exams.

Giving and Viewing Feedback

Teachers can go to the “Members” section and press the blue comment icon to give feedback (text + rating). Students can view feedback from the top-right menu.

If feedback is available, an AI summary option is provided.

Completing an Exam

Inside the course, go to the “Exams” section. You'll see all exams with their status (pending, in progress, completed, or overdue). If the exam hasn’t started, you’ll see a start screen. Otherwise, it opens for completion.

After submitting, the status will update. Completed exams show results when selected. These behaviors also apply to tasks.
